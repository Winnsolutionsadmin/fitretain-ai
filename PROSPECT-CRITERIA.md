# FitRetain Prospect Search Criteria

## Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) Definition

### Primary Target: Independent Gym/Fitness Center Owners

**Business Characteristics:**
- **Business Type:** Independent gyms, fitness centers, boutique studios, martial arts studios
- **Member Count:** 200-1,500 active members
- **Monthly Revenue:** $50K-$300K
- **Staff Size:** 5-25 employees (including part-time trainers)
- **Age of Business:** 2+ years established (past startup survival phase)
- **Locations:** Single or 2-3 location maximum (not large chains)

**Geographic Criteria:**
- **Primary:** Sacramento County, Placer County
- **Secondary:** El Dorado County, Yolo County  
- **Specific Cities:** Sacramento, Folsom, Roseville, Rocklin, Auburn, Davis, Elk Grove
- **Radius:** 45-mile radius from downtown Sacramento

**Technology Adoption Signals:**
- Uses gym management software (Mindbody, Zen Planner, ClubReady, Wodify, Glofox)
- Has business website with online booking/membership info
- Active on social media (Facebook/Instagram business pages)
- Accepts online payments/membership billing
- Uses email marketing tools (MailChimp, Constant Contact, etc.)

### Pain Level Indicators (High-Priority Prospects)

**Financial Stress Signals:**
- Pricing changes or promotional offers on website
- "New Member Specials" frequently advertised
- Multiple membership tiers with discounting
- Social media posts about "growing our family" (acquisition focus)
- Help Wanted postings (high turnover = churn correlation)

**Operational Challenges:**
- Complaints in Google Reviews about billing, scheduling, or communication
- Inconsistent social media posting (resource constraints)
- Website last updated 6+ months ago
- "We're hiring" posts for front desk staff (high turnover)
- Multiple class cancellations due to low attendance

**Competitive Pressure:**
- New franchise gyms opening nearby (Planet Fitness, Anytime Fitness)
- Pricing below market average for area
- Emphasis on "family-owned" or "locally-owned" messaging
- Recent rebranding or facility updates (fighting commoditization)

## Search Methodology

### Database Sources

**Primary Research Tools:**
1. **Google My Business Listings**
   - Search "gyms near Sacramento CA" radius expansion
   - Filter by customer review volume (50+ reviews = established)
   - Note member capacity from photos and descriptions

2. **NAICS Code Targeting (713940 - Fitness and Recreational Sports Centers)**
   - Sacramento County business license databases
   - California business entity search
   - Better Business Bureau member listings

3. **Social Media Intelligence**
   - Facebook business page analysis
   - Instagram business account identification
   - LinkedIn company page targeting

4. **Industry-Specific Directories**
   - IHRSA (International Health, Racquet & Sportsclub Association) member directory
   - Yelp fitness category analysis
   - ClassPass partner gym listings
   - Local fitness competition event sponsor lists

### Data Collection Process

**Step 1: Initial Identification**
Use Google Maps search with terms:
- "gym Sacramento"
- "fitness center Roseville"
- "martial arts studio Folsom"
- "crossfit Auburn"
- "yoga studio Elk Grove"

**Exclusion Criteria:**
- Chain franchises (Planet Fitness, 24 Hour, LA Fitness)
- Corporate-owned facilities
- Recreation centers/community centers
- Home-based personal training
- Outdoor-only bootcamps
- Medical/physical therapy facilities

**Step 2: Qualification Research**
For each prospect, gather:
- Business name and address
- Owner/manager names (LinkedIn, website about page)
- Phone number and email (direct when possible)
- Website URL and last update date
- Member capacity estimates (gym size analysis)
- Current technology stack (scheduler, billing, CRM)
- Social media follower count and engagement
- Google review score and volume
- Pricing information (membership costs)

**Step 3: Pain Level Assessment**
Score each prospect 1-10 on pain likelihood:
- High member turnover indicators (frequent "new member" posts)
- Financial stress signals (constant promotions, price competition)
- Operational challenges (staffing issues, inconsistent posting)
- Technology gaps (outdated website, manual processes)
- Competitive threats (new gyms opening nearby)

## Prospect Segmentation

### Tier 1: Hot Prospects (Contact within 48 hours)
**Characteristics:**
- Clear financial stress indicators
- High pain level score (8-10)
- Recent negative reviews mentioning retention
- Active job postings for customer-facing roles
- Pricing significantly below market average

**Contact Method:** Phone call + LinkedIn connection
**Message:** Direct problem identification with local competitor success story

### Tier 2: Warm Prospects (Contact within 1 week)
**Characteristics:**
- Moderate pain indicators
- Technology adoption readiness
- 5+ years in business (established but potentially stagnating)
- Mid-market pricing positioning
- Moderate social media engagement

**Contact Method:** Email sequence + social media connection
**Message:** Educational content with ROI calculator

### Tier 3: Future Prospects (Nurture sequence)
**Characteristics:**
- Young business (high growth phase)
- Premium pricing positioning
- Strong online presence and engagement
- Recent expansion or renovation
- High review scores (may not feel pain yet)

**Contact Method:** Newsletter subscription + educational content
**Message:** Industry insights and future-proofing strategies

## Sacramento/Placer County Target List

### High-Priority Neighborhoods

**Sacramento County:**
- **Midtown Sacramento:** High competition, price pressure
- **Natomas:** Growing residential, family-oriented gyms
- **Elk Grove:** Suburban expansion area, new gyms opening
- **Rancho Cordova:** Mixed residential/commercial
- **Citrus Heights:** Established community gyms

**Placer County:**
- **Roseville:** Affluent suburb, multiple gym types
- **Rocklin:** Growing family area, boutique fitness trend
- **Auburn:** Smaller market, less competition
- **Folsom:** High-income area, premium fitness focus
- **Lincoln:** Growing market, opportunity for early entry

### Specific Business Types by Area

**Sacramento Urban Core:**
- Crossfit boxes (high member engagement, community focus)
- Martial arts studios (family-oriented, long membership terms)
- Boxing gyms (younger demographic, higher churn risk)
- Boutique fitness studios (specialized classes, premium pricing)

**Suburban Areas (Roseville, Folsom, Elk Grove):**
- Family fitness centers (diverse programming, broad appeal)
- Women-only gyms (Curves-style, strong community aspect)  
- Dance fitness studios (seasonal fluctuation challenges)
- Youth sports training facilities (parent decision makers)

**Industrial/Commercial Areas:**
- 24-hour access gyms (shift worker focused)
- Corporate wellness centers (B2B decision making)
- Rehabilitation-focused fitness (medical integration)

## Prospect Tracking & CRM Setup

### Lead Scoring Model (1-100 Points)

**Pain Level Indicators (40 points maximum):**
- Recent negative reviews mentioning churn/retention (+15)
- Job postings for front desk/member services (+10)
- Frequent promotional pricing changes (+8)
- Social media complaints about billing/service (+7)

**Fit Indicators (30 points maximum):**
- 200-1,500 member capacity (+15)
- Uses gym management software (+10)
- Sacramento/Placer County location (+5)

**Buying Authority (20 points maximum):**
- Owner-operator identified (+15)
- Manager with P&L responsibility (+10)
- Financial decision maker accessibility (+5)

**Timeline Indicators (10 points maximum):**
- Recent technology changes/evaluations (+5)
- Lease renewal period approaching (+3)
- Competitive pressure increasing (+2)
